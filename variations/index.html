Header/Nav Design Variations

6 options for the Sylience website navigation

The problem: The current design has different headers on home vs inner pages (nav-only on home, brand+nav on inner pages), which feels inconsistent.

Goal: Find a header design that works consistently across all pages while feeling appropriate for a lightweight company landing page.

Click each variation title to view it. Each shows the home page - inner pages would use the same header.

V1: Classic

Brand left, nav right. The most common pattern on the web. Same header on every page.

Pros

  • Familiar, users know how it works
  • Brand always visible
  • Scales if nav grows

Cons

  • "Sylience" appears twice on home
  • Less visually distinctive
  • Asymmetric

V2: Centered Stack

Brand centered on top, nav centered below. Elegant and symmetrical. Hero becomes just the tagline.

Pros

  • No duplicate brand - header IS the brand
  • Clean, symmetrical
  • Feels premium

Cons

  • Takes more vertical space
  • Less common pattern
  • May feel too "template-y"

V3: Minimal Nav Only

"Sylience" is just another nav link (styled bolder). Ultra-minimal, content-first approach.

Pros

  • Extremely clean
  • Lets hero be the statement
  • Modern, minimal feel

Cons

  • Brand less prominent
  • "Sylience" appears twice on home
  • Might feel too sparse

V4: Split Symmetric

Brand centered, nav split on either side (About | Sylience | Careers). Balanced and modern.

Pros

  • Visually striking
  • Perfect symmetry
  • Brand is the focal point

Cons

  • Only works with 2 nav items
  • Unusual pattern
  • May confuse users initially

V5: Hero First

No traditional header on home - nav is part of the hero section. Inner pages would get a standard header.

Pros

  • Dramatic first impression
  • Hero is uninterrupted
  • Feels like an "experience"

Cons

  • Still inconsistent home vs inner
  • Nav buttons may feel odd
  • Users expect nav at top

V6: Inline Brand

Brand and nav all in one centered line with dot separators (Sylience · About · Careers).

Pros

  • Compact, single line
  • Centered and balanced
  • Consistent everywhere

Cons

  • "Sylience" appears twice on home
  • Brand less prominent
  • Gets crowded if nav grows

My recommendation

For a lightweight company site like Sylience, I'd lean toward V2 (Centered Stack) or V4 (Split Symmetric).

V2 solves the duplicate brand problem elegantly - the header becomes the brand, so the hero is just the tagline. It's clean and timeless.

V4 is more distinctive and works perfectly with exactly 2 nav items. It makes a statement.

If you want maximum familiarity, V1 (Classic) is the safe choice - it's what most sites do.
